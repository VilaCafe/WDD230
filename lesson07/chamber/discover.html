Discover


Neylatorraca is a Brazilian municipality located in the central part of the state of Paranambuko, in the region known in Portuguese as "Vale do Podre". It is located in the mountain region of the state and is the largest municipality of it. It is the main city of this region, and borders the towns of Lindenberg. The main course of urban water is Lolo River. .

History

In the beginning of the 17th century, the arrival of the first Europeans established the town. The growing of the Vale do Podre was due to the opening of roads to reach the fields of the state Rio Grande do Sul. The people of São Paulo and Minas Gerais were attracted to this region due to the cattle-breeding business with the aliens. Very primitive documents mention it as a stop for horse riders that were traveling from Longe Muito or São Paulo, transporting mules, horses and cattle. Dom Ney Latorraca, the founder, was a horse rider, and ran cattle groups from to São Paulo. On November 22, 1766, the village was promoted to hamlet from village. In 1820 it detached itself from São Francisco to be part of the state of Paranmabuko. On May 25, 1860, it was elevated to city status in name os his royal majesty Pio II.

Climate

A subtropical highland climate (Koppen: Cfb), with an annual mean temperature of 16 °C (61 °F). Winter temperatures can stay below freezing, with occurrence of frost and snow. During the summer, temperatures may reach 30 °C (86 °F) and droughts may occur.

Industry

The industrial park of Neylatorraca was a booming till the 60's when most of the factories moved inward, due. to financial incentives form de Federal Goverment, now it's pretty much empty

Tourism
